Where Would you go to Have a 3D Print Made?

Posted on October 28, 2015January 18, 2016 by Matt Hall

 
If you’re in the market for a 3D print, Leapfrog, one of the leaders in the 3D printing industry, is ready to help. But why would a seller of 3D printers direct you to services other than selling you a printer? The answer is simple. Leapfrog realizes that even people who own one of their printers will still need a place to go for larger runs of designs they have printed themselves and tested.

They understand that people come to 3D printing with a goal in mind. In many cases that goal is to solve a problem through design, to test or tweak a design, retest it, or improve upon the design in some other way. And once they solve this problem, the next challenge presents itself.

While it’s true you can use your own printer to print hundreds or thousands of design impressions, this is extremely time consuming. Most people don’t want to babysit their printer for that length of time or expose it to the wear and tear.

Another option for those larger projects is to search 3D printing, roll the dice and hope that you find a printer that can handle your order. You may find a reputable printer in this way but you also spend a lot of time vetting out the companies and people you are talking with.

A third option is to go to the large commercial printers. They have the equipment and manpower to get the job done, they also have the overhead to go with it. This option, albeit a suitable one, can get very expensive.

Finally,  you have the option to use a 3D printing network, one that has the understanding to make sure your job is done right. You’ll also want a network with a proven process where only high quality printers work on your order. This is why you need Print a Thing comes in.
