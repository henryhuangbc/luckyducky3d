Here’s an Interesting Look at Crowd-Sourced 3D Printing

Posted on September 30, 2015April 27, 2016 by Matt Hall

One thing we admire about the 3D printing universe is the many people that love to share what they have learned. It’s that support of one another that has been one of the key reasons for the growth of the 3D printing interesting. Tarun Tampi is a good example of that sharing. Back in March he wrote an interesting article about the 3D printing marketplace, specifically the crowd-sourced 3D printing services that were available. You can read the full story here.

3D printer hard at work

Tarun does a great job of highlighting one of the problems in the crowd-sourced space which is the back and forth between the designer and the supplier. At Print a Thing we strive to make the process easier for both parties by handing all of the communication that needs to take place to get your print done correctly. We strive to be, as Tarun put it, “a 3D printing concierge service.” We do this because we know that if Print a Thing has a high level of service we are going to attract higher level providers to our network and ultimately, more customers and opportunities to share in this growing industry.

To read more from Tarun Tampi check out his author page.
